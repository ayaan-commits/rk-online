## What Nobody Tells You About Moving to Kazakhstan

You've seen the brochures. You've heard the consultant pitch. But what's it actually like to live in Kokshetau as an 18-year-old Indian student? Here's the unfiltered version.

## The First Week

The first week is the hardest. You'll land at the airport, likely tired and nervous. If you're coming through RK Online, someone from our team will pick you up and take you to the hostel. Your room will be ready.

The time difference is only 1.5 hours ahead of IST, so jet lag isn't really a thing. But the landscape will feel different — wide open spaces, cleaner air, and a lot of sky.

## Your Hostel

Kokshetau State University has 5 residential halls. Here's what you actually get:

- **Shared rooms**: Usually 2-3 students per room
- **Furniture**: Bed, desk, wardrobe, chair — basic but functional
- **Internet**: Free Wi-Fi (decent speed for video calls home)
- **Heating**: Central heating — your room stays warm even when it's -20°C outside
- **Kitchen**: Shared kitchen on each floor (this is where the magic happens — more on food below)
- **Laundry**: Available on campus
- **Security**: 24/7 guards, CCTV, controlled entry

Is it a 5-star hotel? No. Is it safe, clean, and livable? Yes.

## Food — The Big Question

Let's be honest: this is the #1 concern for every Indian parent.

**The hostel mess** serves a mix of Kazakh, Russian, and international food. You'll find rice, bread, soups, and salads. It's edible and nutritious, but it won't taste like home.

**The real solution**: most Indian students cook. The shared kitchens become mini Indian restaurants every evening. You'll learn to make dal, rice, sabzi, and even parathas. Indian spices, atta, and dal are available at local stores (there's a whole Indian grocery supply chain because of the 500+ students).

**Indian restaurants**: There are a few in the city. Not on every corner, but enough that you won't starve for biryani.

**RK Online tip**: We send students with a "survival kit" — masala dabba, pressure cooker, and basic spices. You'd be surprised how fast you learn to cook.

## The Weather (It's Cold, Yes)

Let's not pretend — Kazakhstan gets cold. Really cold.

- **Winter (Nov-Mar)**: -15°C to -25°C. Snow everywhere. You NEED a proper winter jacket, thermal layers, and boots.
- **Spring (Apr-May)**: 5°C to 15°C. Nice, melting snow, green starts appearing.
- **Summer (Jun-Aug)**: 25°C to 35°C. Genuinely pleasant. Students go out, play cricket, explore.
- **Autumn (Sep-Oct)**: 10°C to 20°C. Beautiful colors, comfortable weather.

The cold is manageable because:
1. Every building has central heating — you're never cold indoors
2. You adapt within the first winter
3. Online shopping for winter gear is easy

## Daily Routine of an MBBS Student

A typical day looks like:

- **8:00 AM**: Wake up, breakfast (hostel mess or self-cooked)
- **9:00 AM - 2:00 PM**: Classes (lectures + lab sessions)
- **2:00 PM**: Lunch break
- **3:00 PM - 5:00 PM**: Clinical sessions / self-study
- **5:00 PM - 7:00 PM**: Free time (gym, cricket, video calls home)
- **7:00 PM**: Dinner (usually cooked with friends)
- **8:00 PM - 11:00 PM**: Study time
- **11:00 PM**: Sleep

Weekends are flexible — shopping, exploring the city, catching up on assignments, or just sleeping in.

## Safety

Parents, this is for you.

Kokshetau is a small, peaceful university city. It's not a major metropolis with big-city problems. The university campus has:

- 24/7 security guards at all entrances
- CCTV cameras across the campus
- Controlled hostel entry (visitors are logged)
- A dedicated international student support office
- Emergency helpline numbers

In 3+ years of sending students here, we haven't had a single serious safety incident. That said, common sense applies everywhere — don't wander alone late at night in unfamiliar areas, keep your documents safe, and stay connected with the student community.

## The Indian Community

This is probably the best part. With 500+ Indian students at Kokshetau:

- **Festivals**: Diwali, Holi, Eid, Christmas — all celebrated with proper decorations, food, and parties
- **Cricket**: Yes, there are cricket games. Students bring bats from India.
- **Study groups**: Seniors help juniors with subjects, exam prep, and FMGE preparation
- **WhatsApp groups**: Separate groups for each year, housing, food, emergencies
- **Seniors**: The senior student network is incredibly strong. They'll guide you through everything from registration to the best place to buy groceries.

## Communication with Home

- **Video calls**: Free Wi-Fi + WhatsApp = unlimited video calls with family
- **Indian SIM**: Not needed. Get a local Kazakh SIM for ₹500, use WhatsApp for everything
- **Time difference**: Only 1.5 hours ahead of IST — you can call family at normal times

## Coming Home

Students typically come home 2-3 times per year:
- Winter break (December-January)
- Summer break (June-August)
- Sometimes mid-semester breaks

Flight options vary, but connecting flights through Delhi/Mumbai are common. Budget ₹30,000-50,000 per round trip.

## The Honest Bottom Line

Living in Kazakhstan as an Indian student is not a vacation. It's a serious commitment to your medical education in a different country with a different culture and climate.

But it's also an adventure. You'll grow up fast, learn to cook, make lifelong friends from across India (and the world), and come back as a doctor.

The students who do well here are the ones who come with realistic expectations and an open mind. If you're expecting a 5-star experience, you'll be disappointed. If you're expecting an affordable, quality medical education with a strong support system — that's exactly what you'll get.
